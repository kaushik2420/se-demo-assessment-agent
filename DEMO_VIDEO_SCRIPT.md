# SE Coach Portal — Demo Video Script

**Target length:** 2:30 – 3:00.
**Tool:** Loom (free, fastest) or QuickTime Player (Mac, no signup). Either captures screen + your voice.
**Recording surface:** the **prototype HTML** (`se_portal_prototype.html`) — has the click-through flow with sample data, perfect for a clean recording with no real prospect/SE data exposed.
**Voice:** conversational, fast, no scripted reading. Hit the beats below in your own words.

---

## Before you start recording (1 minute setup)

1. Open `se_portal_prototype.html` in Chrome at **125% zoom** (so text reads cleanly in the recording).
2. Quit any apps that might notify you mid-recording (Slack, Mail).
3. Open Loom → "Screen + Camera" → choose **Tab** capture (not full screen), select the prototype tab, **Cam in bottom-right small circle**.
4. Hide your bookmarks bar (Cmd+Shift+B). Close other tabs.
5. Do one practice run-through of the click path below (no recording) so you know the flow.

---

## SCENE 1 — Opening (0:00 – 0:15) · 15 seconds

**Screen:** Login screen of the prototype (with the navy + teal gradient background and SurveySparrow logo card).

**Say (paraphrase):**

> "Hey team — quick 3-minute walkthrough of the SE Coach portal we're rolling out next week. This is the tool that scores every demo you do, gives you a coaching action for the month, and shows where you stand against the broader SaaS SE industry. Let me show you what you'll see when you log in."

**Action:** Click the **Sign in** button (no need to type credentials in the prototype).

---

## SCENE 2 — Your dashboard (0:15 – 1:15) · 60 seconds

**Screen:** SE dashboard for Ishrath, showing the 4 hero metric cards, coaching action card, and recent calls list.

**Say:**

> "This is your dashboard. Four things to know — top to bottom.
>
> [point to top-left card] Your current score, out of 5. This is your weighted average across all the calls you uploaded this month.
>
> [point to top-right percentile card] Your industry percentile. P10 means roughly the bottom 10% of B2B SaaS SEs; P50 is the median; P75 is the top quarter. We're benchmarking against Gartner, PreSales Collective, Bain data — not just against this team. There's a full methodology doc if you want to dive deep.
>
> [scroll to coaching action card] **This is the most important thing on the page.** Your single coaching action for the month. One behavior to change. We deliberately don't give you a list of 10 things to fix — one focused change sustained over a month moves you up a percentile band.
>
> [scroll to recent calls] And below, every call you uploaded this month. Click any one to see the deep dive."

**Action:** Click the first call (BTS) in the Recent Calls list.

---

## SCENE 3 — Call detail (1:15 – 2:15) · 60 seconds

**Screen:** Call detail page showing per-criterion scores, top 3 strengths, top 3 gaps, the 9 deal-intelligence signals.

**Say:**

> "Here's what we capture for every call. Four headline stats up top — your weighted score, percentile, your selling style this call (feature-seller vs value-seller), and a flag for whether your AE behaved well.
>
> [point to criterion breakdown on left] Score by criterion. The bar shows where you land on the 0 to 5 scale. The little 'gap' number shows where you sit vs the SaaS industry median for that specific criterion. Solution Skills minus 1.75 — that's the biggest leverage area to focus on.
>
> [scroll to strengths/gaps on right] Top 3 strengths and top 3 gaps. Every one of these is evidence-based — Claude points at a specific moment in your transcript when it picks a strength or a gap. So you can listen back and learn from the actual interaction.
>
> [scroll down to the 9 insights grid] And this part is for the deal, not for you. Nine signals we pull from every call — what the prospect actually wants, where they are on the CX maturity curve, what features they're asking for, who they're comparing us to, where the deal is at risk. The CEO sees these aggregated to spot product and process gaps every month."

**Action:** Click "Back to dashboard".

---

## SCENE 4 — Upload flow (2:15 – 2:45) · 30 seconds

**Screen:** Click "+ Upload transcript" → Upload modal opens.

**Say:**

> "Last thing — how you upload. Hit this button, pick the call type — and call type matters, because we score POCs differently from closure calls — paste your transcript from Granola, hit Analyze. About 45 seconds later you're back at your dashboard with a fresh scorecard."

**Action:** Click "Try a transcript" sample button → shows green ✓ validation. Then click "Try meeting notes (rejected)" → shows red ✗ with reason.

> "Quick note — we accept transcripts only, not meeting notes. If you paste notes or a summary, we'll reject it with a clear reason. The validator works on Granola, Avoma, Otter, Fathom — we auto-detect the format."

---

## SCENE 5 — Close (2:45 – 3:00) · 15 seconds

**Screen:** Close the modal, back on dashboard.

**Say:**

> "Two things I want from you. One — install Granola today and keep it running on every external call. Two — upload your first transcript this week so we have baseline data to coach against. Full setup instructions are in the onboarding doc I'm dropping in the SE Slack. Ping me with any questions."

**Action:** End recording.

---

## Post-production checklist

- Trim the first/last 2 seconds (typically wobble while you start/stop recording)
- Add a 2-second intro card: **"SurveySparrow SE Coach — 3-minute walkthrough"** + the SurveySparrow logo (Loom has built-in title cards)
- Loom auto-generates a transcript and a shareable URL — copy both
- Post in **#se-team** Slack with the Loom link, pinned to the channel
- Add to the rollout email as the first line: "**90-second TL;DR:** [link]. Full walkthrough below."

---

## Optional: pre-recorded inline narration for the prototype

If you'd rather not record yourself talking, an alternative is **a silent screen capture** with on-screen text callouts at each scene. Slower to produce but works for async audiences. Tools: **Tella.tv** or **Arcade.software** (both have generous free tiers and produce polished GIFs/MP4s with annotation overlays).

If you go this route, the script above doubles as your text-callout sequence — every "Say:" block becomes an on-screen caption that appears for 4-5 seconds.
